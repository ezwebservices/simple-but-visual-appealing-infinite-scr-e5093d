### Email 2: First Session Tips (Day 1)

**Subject:** 3 things to know before your child's first swipe

**Preview text:** Tiny tips that make a big difference.

**Body:**

Hi!

Your child's about to have their first NumPals session. Here's what to expect:

**1. Turn the volume up.**
Sound is the main interface. Every number, every instruction, every celebration is audio. NumPals was designed ears-first.

**2. Let them explore freely.**
There's no wrong way to use it. Every card works independently. If they swipe fast, they'll slow down when something catches their eye. If they tap the wrong answer, they'll hear "Almost! Try again!" — never a harsh buzzer.

**3. Watch for character favorites.**
Kids form real attachments to the NumPals. When your child starts asking for Sunny or Rosie by name, that's the engagement that drives learning.

**Session length tip:** 5-10 minutes is perfect at this age. But don't stress about it — the app has no addictive hooks, just natural stopping points.

[Start swiping]

— The NumPals Team

## Sequence 4: Parent Education

---

### Email 1: The Science of Play-Based Math (Day 10)

**Subject:** Why your child learns more from Sunny the Lion than from flashcards

**Preview text:** The research behind play-based early math.

**Body:**

Hi!

You might wonder: does swiping through animal adventures actually teach math? Here's what the research says:

**Play-based learning outperforms direct instruction at this age.** When children are intrinsically motivated (playing with characters they love), retention is significantly higher than worksheet-style practice.

**Multi-sensory input accelerates learning.** NumPals combines visual (colorful scenes), auditory (narration + sound effects), and tactile (tapping + swiping) input. This multi-channel approach maps to how young brains build number sense.

**Positive emotional associations matter.** Children who associate math with joy and play develop stronger math confidence. That's why we never show a red X — every interaction ends with encouragement.

**Repetition through variety.** Infinite scroll means kids practice the same concepts across dozens of different visual scenarios. Same skill, new context, every time.

Your child isn't just playing. They're building the foundation for every math concept that comes next.

[Continue the adventure]

— The NumPals Team

## Email Design Notes

- **Header:** NumPals logo with one character illustration (rotate characters across emails)
- **Colors:** Bright, warm palette matching character colors (blue, yellow, pink, orange, teal)
- **Typography:** Nunito or Quicksand for body text (matching brand guide)
- **CTA buttons:** Large, rounded, colorful (mimicking in-app design language)
- **Footer:** Minimal — unsubscribe link, no social media clutter
- **Tone:** Warm, encouraging, brief. Mirror the app's voice: playful but not childish (these emails are for parents).
