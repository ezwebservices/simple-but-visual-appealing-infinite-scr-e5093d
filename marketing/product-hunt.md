# NumPals — Product Hunt Launch

## Tagline

**Swipe, Count, Roar! TikTok-style math for preschoolers.**

## One-Liner

NumPals turns infinite scroll into a superpower — toddlers swipe through bite-sized math lessons starring lovable animal characters, with zero reading required.

---

## Description

**What if TikTok was built for 4-year-olds learning math?**

NumPals is a full-screen, infinite-scroll math app designed entirely for preschoolers (ages 3-5). Every swipe reveals a colorful, animated card where animal friends teach counting, addition, and subtraction through play — not worksheets.

### How it works

- **Swipe to learn.** Each card is a 10-15 second micro-lesson. Kids scroll naturally, just like they see adults do.
- **Animal characters guide every lesson.** Meet Bloo the Bear (counting), Sunny the Lion (addition), Rosie the Bunny (subtraction), Milo the Monkey (mixed challenges), and Pip the Penguin (number recognition).
- **Sound-first design.** Every number is spoken aloud. Every tap plays a satisfying pop, boing, or squeak. Audio narrates everything — no reading needed.
- **Celebrate everything.** Confetti, bouncing animals, ascending chimes. Wrong answers get a gentle "Almost! Try again!" — never a red X.
- **Built for tiny fingers.** Chunky 64px+ touch targets, rounded shapes, full-screen scenes with zero nav clutter.

### Why parents love it

- No ads. No external links. No text input. No social features.
- Screen time that genuinely teaches counting 1-20 and intro addition/subtraction (sums to 10).
- Optional parent dashboard for progress tracking.
- Polished, warm design that feels safe — like a favorite stuffed animal.

### Why kids love it

They don't even know they're learning. They're just playing with their animal pals, tapping frogs, panda-counting, and frog-jumping to five. Math just happens.

## First Comment (Maker)

Hey Product Hunt! We're the team behind NumPals.

We built this because we saw a gap: there are hundreds of kids' math apps, but almost all of them feel like worksheets with cartoon paint. We wanted something that *felt* like play from the very first swipe.

The infinite scroll format was inspired by how naturally kids already swipe on phones and tablets. Instead of fighting that behavior, we leaned into it — every swipe is a reward, every card is a tiny adventure with an animal friend.

Everything is designed for a 4-year-old to navigate completely alone:
- No text menus
- No login screens
- No "ask your parent" gates blocking the fun

We spent months on the audio and character design because at this age, sound IS the interface. Bloo the Bear's calm voice for counting, Sunny the Lion's excited roar for addition — kids form real bonds with these characters.

We'd love your feedback. What would make this even better for your little ones?
