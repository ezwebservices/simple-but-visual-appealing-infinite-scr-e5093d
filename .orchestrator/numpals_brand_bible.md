# NumPals Visual Brand Bible — v2 ("Number Realm")

**Audience:** Pre-K (ages 3–5) and their parents.
**Brief in one line:** Keep the names, ditch the plushy softness. Rebuild the cast as a *Nintendo-grade ensemble* — bold, confident, collectible — that can anchor a multi-season series.

---

## 1. Art Direction

We are moving from **"plush toy on a pastel background"** to **"hero cast of a Switch-era adventure game."** Think Splatoon's confidence, Pokemon's silhouette discipline, Animal Crossing's warmth — not Saturday-morning fluff.

### Line & shape
- **Bold, unbroken outlines.** 2.5–3.5px weight at standard size; taper at joints, never disappear. Think Splatoon/Zelda Tears-of-the-Kingdom ink contour.
- **Confident geometry.** Primary shapes read in 3 beats max: head-mass, body-mass, accent. No mushy curves — every arc has intent.
- **Expressive asymmetry.** One pose-hand forward, weight on one foot, head tilted. Static T-poses are forbidden.
- **Chunky proportions** — roughly 3 to 3.5 heads tall. Big head, big eyes, solid stance. Feet plant the character.

### Color
- **Saturated but disciplined.** Each NumPal owns one signature hue + one accent. Supporting tones desaturate around them; the hero color is always the loudest thing on screen.
- **Flat-shade + one drop-shadow layer.** No soft airbrush, no plush fuzz gradients. Cel-shading with a single mid-tone and a single shadow — that's it.
- **Light from upper-left.** Consistent across the cast so group shots read as a team.

### Energy
- Cool, capable, *in on the joke*. They wink. They pose. They know they're the stars.
- Never scary, never edgy-as-in-dark. "Edgy" here = **confident, stylized, collectible** — the vibe a 4-year-old gets from a Pokemon starter choice, not the vibe from a horror game.

## 2. The Series Framing — "Number Realm"

The NumPals are the founding six of the **Number Realm**, a world built out of arithmetic itself — bridges made of digits, rivers that count themselves, a sky that rearranges into equations at dusk. Each NumPal is a *Guardian* of one core idea (counting, shapes, patterns, etc.) and together they form **Team Prime**.

**Why this matters for design:** any future character — season 2, 3, spin-offs — must answer three questions:
1. **Which realm-region do they come from?** (Counting Coast, Shape Steppe, Pattern Pines, Measure Mesa, Logic Lagoon, etc.)
2. **What math idea do they Guard?**
3. **Do they carry the Realm Mark?** — a tiny glowing numeral somewhere on their design (collar, visor, palm, shell). This is our *signature silhouette language* — the one unifier across every character we'll ever ship.

### Silhouette language (the throughline)
- **Round-top, planted-bottom.** Head/hood/helm is always the most rounded element; feet are always grounded and squared-off. You should recognize a NumPal from 30 feet away just from the bean-on-boots silhouette.
- **One "hero asymmetry"** per character — a scarf, single shoulder pad, tail curl, antenna bend. Breaks the symmetry and makes them toy-shelf legible.
- **The Realm Mark** — a single digit glyph glowing faintly on their gear. Pre-K kids learn to spot it as the "you're one of the good guys" badge.

### Factions (seeded now, payoff later)
- **Team Prime** — our six. Guardians.
- **The Remainders** — mischief crew (future season). Not villains — rivals.
- **The Zero Court** — neutral elders (future season).

Designer doesn't need to draw Remainders or Zero Court yet. Just know the gap exists so Team Prime's design leaves room to contrast *against* something later.

## 3. Cast Breakdown

Names are locked. Identities below are the **v2 rework.** Roughly half the cast is pushed toward humanoid / unique-character territory; the rest stay creature or hybrid so the ensemble reads varied on a toy-shelf group shot.

### BlooBear — the Captain *(Humanoid)*
- **Archetype:** Humanoid. Teen-coded hero in a bear-hooded jacket. The hood *is* the bear — ears up top, muzzle forming the brim. Face underneath is a confident kid with a crooked grin.
- **Role in Team Prime:** Captain. Guardian of **Counting**.
- **Personality:** Easygoing leader. Thinks out loud. Never panics. Fist-bump energy.
- **Signature prop / power:** The **Tally Gauntlet** — a fingerless glove that lights up one numeral at a time as he counts things in the world.
- **Palette:** Ice-blue hoodie (#7AA5C7 hero), cream trim, deep navy outline. Tally Gauntlet glows warm gold.
- **Silhouette hook:** The ear-tips on the hood.

### SunnyBug — the Scout *(Creature)*
- **Archetype:** Creature. Stays a bug — but redrawn as a **stylized rhino-beetle-meets-ladybug**, not plush. Hard shell, articulated legs, glossy wings tucked under elytra.
- **Role in Team Prime:** Scout. Guardian of **Patterns** (because bugs track repetition).
- **Personality:** Fast, curious, says what she sees. The first to dart toward a problem.
- **Signature prop / power:** **Pattern Wings** — when she opens them, the spot-pattern on her elytra is the answer to the current sequence.
- **Palette:** Coral-orange hero (#E2806A), ink-black spots, cream underbelly. Wings iridescent when open.
- **Silhouette hook:** Horn-stub on the head + distinct elytra split.

### RosieOwl — the Strategist *(Humanoid)*
- **Archetype:** Humanoid. A calm kid in an **owl-feather cloak** with a soft hood that reads as owl-head from a distance. Carries herself like a young scholar-knight.
- **Role in Team Prime:** Strategist. Guardian of **Logic & Sorting**.
- **Personality:** Observant, quiet, dry humor. Thinks three moves ahead. Says the smart thing once, not twice.
- **Signature prop / power:** **The Ledger Lantern** — a hand-held lantern whose light reveals the right answer only when her reasoning is sound.
- **Palette:** Dusty rose + plum feathers (#E07898 hero, #9D5870 shadow), cream under-feathers, brass lantern accents.
- **Silhouette hook:** Tall, pointed hood-ears + cloak tail that curls at the hem.

### MiloFrog — the Athlete *(Hybrid)*
- **Archetype:** Hybrid. Still frog-bodied but bipedal and kitted out — **amphibian track-kid**. Sweatband, sleeveless tee, taped fingers. Reads like a Splatoon Inkling in frog form.
- **Role in Team Prime:** Athlete. Guardian of **Measurement** (jumps, distances, time).
- **Personality:** Hype machine. Everything is a challenge. Good sport when he loses.
- **Signature prop / power:** **Leap-Meter Sneakers** — each jump draws a glowing number in the air showing distance/height.
- **Palette:** Kelly green (#5BA868 hero), chartreuse belly, white sweatband, hot-pink sneaker laces as the asymmetry accent.
- **Silhouette hook:** One ear-fin bent, headband tails flying.

### PipZebra — the Speedster *(Humanoid)*
- **Archetype:** Humanoid. A wiry kid in a **zebra-stripe racing jacket and helmet** — visor down, ponytail out the back. Think pocket-racer energy.
- **Role in Team Prime:** Speedster. Guardian of **Sequencing & Order** (first, next, last).
- **Personality:** Quick-witted, impatient, loyal. Finishes others' sentences. Always first to the scene.
- **Signature prop / power:** **Stripe-Dash** — when she runs, her stripes trail behind as a numbered track showing the order of steps.
- **Palette:** Crisp white + ink black (#A8B0BC / #0A0A12), a single electric-magenta visor stripe (#FF3D9A) as the hero pop.
- **Silhouette hook:** The visor + trailing ponytail.

### RexRobot — the Inventor *(Humanoid-mech)*
- **Archetype:** Humanoid mech. A compact kid-shaped robot, not a boxy toy. Articulated joints, exposed wiring at elbows/knees, a faceplate that shows expressive LED eyes + mouth.
- **Role in Team Prime:** Inventor. Guardian of **Shapes & Geometry**.
- **Personality:** Earnest, literal, warm once you know him. Collects facts. Says "affirmative" unironically.
- **Signature prop / power:** **Chest Display** — a geometric HUD that projects shapes he can then manipulate in the air.
- **Palette:** Cyan plating (#6DC8DD hero), white panels, exposed dark-slate joints, amber LED face.
- **Silhouette hook:** Single bent antenna + chest panel glow.

### Cast balance check
- **Humanoid:** BlooBear, RosieOwl, PipZebra, RexRobot (mech) — 4
- **Hybrid:** MiloFrog — 1
- **Creature:** SunnyBug — 1

Four out of six read as unique human-like characters; two hold the "creature/hybrid" line so the ensemble doesn't flatten into a school-class group. This is the ratio — do not drift away from it.

## 4. Rules for the Designer

### Line weight
- **Primary outline:** 3px (±0.5) at 512×512 render. Never break.
- **Interior detail lines:** 1.5px max. Used sparingly — face, prop seams, one or two gear lines.
- No double-outlines, no sketchy hatching.

### Shading
- **Two tones per surface.** Base + one shadow. Optional single highlight as a hard-edged shape, never a gradient.
- **Light source:** upper-left, 45°.
- **Drop shadow under feet:** soft oval, 25% opacity, cast on the ground plane.
- No ambient occlusion, no gradient fills inside body panels, no noise/texture overlays.

### Silhouette test
Before finalizing any character:
1. Fill the whole figure 100% black on a white background.
2. At 64×64 px, the character must be **uniquely identifiable** from the other five.
3. If two silhouettes are confusable, adjust the "hero asymmetry" element until they aren't.

### "Edgy" for Pre-K — what it means here
- ✅ Confident pose, slight smirk, cool gear, stylized proportions.
- ✅ Bold saturated color, high contrast, crisp lines.
- ✅ Hero-shot energy — like they're on the game box, not a daycare poster.
- ❌ No fangs, claws, weapons, skulls, blood/ink drips, scars, smoke, flames as threat.
- ❌ No sulking, scowling, or "too cool for you" energy. They're *into it*, just stylized.
- ❌ No visible tech looks "broken" or "glitchy" — keeps it safe for anxious kids.

### Safe motifs (use freely)
Numerals, shapes, constellations, ribbons, glow lines, soft lightning (as *energy* not storm), stars, arrows, medal/badge shapes, racing stripes, gears.

### Unsafe motifs (do not use)
Fangs, horns-as-weapons, blades, guns/blasters even stylized, chains, bandages, bruises, monster-eyes, sharp spikes, hooded-figure-face-hidden silhouettes, anything occult.

### Consistency rules across the cast
- Same eye-highlight treatment on every character (two white dots, same relative position).
- Same mouth language: three states — smile, open-cheer, focused-line. Nothing more complex in the base set.
- Every character carries the **Realm Mark** (a faintly-glowing numeral) somewhere on their gear. Find a home for it per character and note it on the turnaround.

### Deliverables per character (for v2 rework)
1. Front turnaround at 1024×1024 (hero pose, not T-pose).
2. Three face states: smile / cheer / focused.
3. Silhouette test PNG at 64×64.
4. Palette swatches with hex codes.
5. One "team shot" composition with all six in hero stance.

## 5. What "anchors a series" means here

If we do this right, the six NumPals above are the **Gen 1 starter page** of a universe that scales. Seasons add:
- New realm-regions (Shape Steppe, Measure Mesa, Logic Lagoon…).
- Rival faction (The Remainders) with *inverted* silhouette cues — flat-top, pointy-bottom — so they contrast instantly on screen.
- Collectible side-pals that live in single regions.

Designer: lock Gen 1 first. Don't draw ahead. But draw it knowing a Gen 2 is coming, and leave room in the palette system and silhouette grammar for them.
